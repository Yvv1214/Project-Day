1. Koyasan

Koyasan, one of Japan's sacred mountain, is considered one the best places to visit during a visit to Japan. Located at Mount Koya You get to experience Japan's religous traditions in person during the daily morning prayers. It has a beautiful scenery even when its snowing and you get to see one of Japan's largest rock gardent. As well as tour the ancient cementary where Kobo Daishi, the founder of Shingon school of Japanese Buddhism, is said to be at eternal meditation. As a tourist you have the option to stay overnight at the Budhist Temple. There they serve a variety of vegetarian cuisine as monks are vegetarian themselves.

2. Kyoto

Kyoto is one the cities that was not bombeb during WWII. As a tourist one can not fully explore Kyoto as it has so much to offer and so much to see. The bamboo forest Arashiyama has sky high bamboos providing a shady trail where many touris walk though to enjoy the ambience it provides. Then there is the Shirakawa River, said to be one of the most beautiful river in Asia, is beffitted cherry cherry blossoms. For those who travel for food theres the Nishiki Market where vendors sell just about anything imaginable from octopus on stick to deep fried chocolate balls, tofu and seaweed. Kyoto also has Geisha shows where they perform on dances on stage. Though they are expensive and it is a rare sight to see them out in the open. Finally, Fushimi Inari, a trail where you can walk through thousands of torri gates, is a shrine dedicated to the Shinto god of rice. While it may be a long walk, there are rest areas containing restaurant and sight seeing.

3. Tokyo

If you want to experience modern Japan, Tokyo is the place. Tokyo has many scenic places as well as bizarre things to do. There is so much to do and see there. From riding go karts in Mario Kart style in the streets to canoeing at night at Koto ward. There are many interesting restaurants and hotels as well. Such as the Robot, restaurant where you're surrounded by robots putting on a show and Ninja Akasaka, where you walk through a passage way where ninjas sneak up on you as you make your way inside the ninja themed restaurant. For the hotels they have a Godzilla themed hotel and Park Hotel, which has very artistic rooms. Here are some more bizarre hotels and restaurants. Fun fact, Tokyo has vending machines everwhere that sell just about everthing from ramen, burgers, groceries, and clothing. For those who want to relax and enjoy nature for a bit there is a beautiful garden that is 55 square miles big, called Rikugien.
